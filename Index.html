Daniel 11 — Exhaustive Teaching Commentary

Daniel 11:2–4

Verse Reference: Persian kings and rise of Greece

Step 1: Persian culmination under Xerxes I
Xerxes I amasses vast wealth and launches campaigns against Greece, escalating East-West conflict.
Step 2: Rise of Alexander the Great
Alexander defeats Persia decisively, shifting global dominance to the Greek world.
Step 3: Death and fragmentation
After Alexander's sudden death, no unified successor emerges.
Step 4: Division of empire
The empire splits among generals, forming Seleucid and Ptolemaic powers.
Fulfillment Summary: Transition from Persia to Greece and fragmentation
Confidence: Very High

Daniel 11:5–9

Step 1: Emergence of two powers
Ptolemy I (Egypt) and Seleucus I (Syria) dominate post-Alexander territories.
Step 2: Strategic conflict
Judea becomes a contested region due to its geopolitical importance.
Step 3: War cycle
Ongoing Syrian Wars define relations for generations.
Fulfillment Summary: Long-term geopolitical struggle
Confidence: High

Daniel 11:10–12

Step 1: Antiochus III campaigns
Seleucid expansion southward intensifies conflict.
Step 2: Battle of Raphia
Egypt wins tactically but fails to maintain control.
Fulfillment Summary: Temporary Egyptian success
Confidence: Very High

Daniel 11:21–24

Step 1: Rise of Antiochus IV Epiphanes
Seizes power through intrigue rather than rightful succession.
Step 2: Consolidation
Strengthens centralized control and political authority.
Fulfillment Summary: Authoritarian consolidation
Confidence: Very High

Daniel 11:31–35

Step 1: Temple desecration
Jewish sacrificial system halted under Antiochus IV.
Step 2: Maccabean Revolt
Jewish resistance led by the Maccabees restores worship.
Fulfillment Summary: Religious persecution and restoration
Confidence: Very High

Daniel 11:36–39

Step 1: Self-exalting ruler
King elevates himself above traditional gods, possibly Antiochus IV or symbolic figure.
Fulfillment Summary: Ideological kingship
Confidence: Medium

Daniel 11:40–45

Step 1: Final conflicts
Narrative shifts into less historically precise, possibly apocalyptic territory.
Fulfillment Summary: Symbolic/apocalyptic culmination
Confidence: Low
